CanSat



Situation


I participated in a CanSat competition at the University Space Program, and the objective was to create a Satellite with the size of a soda can, this is why its name “CanSat”, and this should have specific dimensions, specific height, and weight measurements, also, the most important part is that it should contain a passenger, and It was simulated by an egg, it should survive to a free fall impact. The CanSat was launched of 130m approximately, and it should send pressure, temperature, speed, and altitude signals to an earth station. Also, we had a limited budget of $1,000 MXN



Task


My role was guaranteeing the protection of the passenger, and the damping system to protect the CanSat outside, this should stand the impact.



Action


After made an Ishikawa Diagram and identify the causes of our problems, we found that the principal problem was that the egg was breaking inside the CanSat and that made the electrical system fail, so all the CanSat failed I had to find a better distribution for each system, I had to protect and isolate the egg. I was looking for materials that were strong enough and light enough, also, it should be cheap, one day I found in my house, moldable foamy, the foamy is very light and cheap, so I decided to cover the egg with it and for more protection I cover the egg with bubble wrap. With the outside structure of the CanSat I decided to use carton because of its lightness and for the damping system I used a piece of rubber, incredibly, I took this piece of rubber from a mat in my house. I did a lot of testes with this design, all was good, the passenger was surviving to the impact.



Results


It was the launch day and unfortunately it didn’t survive, it broke, but the liquid didn’t affect the other systems, and the outside structure only had a small dent.Regardless my team and I received the 4th place of the competition. My reflection of this is that even you don’t succeed there is always some knowledge, and in the future, you can do it better.
